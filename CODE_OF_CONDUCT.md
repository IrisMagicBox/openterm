# Code Of Conduct

OpenTerm should be a practical, respectful place to collaborate.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Assume good intent, but accept correction when something lands badly.
- Focus criticism on code, behavior, documentation, and design decisions.
- Keep discussions useful for people trying to build, debug, or use the project.
- Respect user safety, privacy, credentials, and remote systems.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, personal attacks, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Encouraging unsafe terminal commands without clear context and safeguards.
- Deliberately misleading reports, reviews, or instructions.
- Repeatedly derailing technical discussions.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments; close issues; reject contributions; or restrict participation when behavior harms the project or its users.

If you need to report a conduct issue, contact the maintainers privately with relevant context.
